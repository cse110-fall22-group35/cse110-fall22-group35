Writing-tips #1

Start with a couple of sentences describing the book

Avoid giving spoilers or major plot twists of the book. As a general rule, avoid mentioning in detail major events from middle of the book onwards. Mention if the book is part of a series, especially if one should read the other books to enjoy this one.

Writing-tips #2

Discuss what you particularly liked about the book

Focus your thoughts and feelings about the story and how it was told. Here are some questions you can answer:

    Who was your favorite character? Why?
    Did the characters feel real to you? Or 2-Dimensional? Why?
    Did the story keep you guessing?
    What was your favorite part of the book? Why?
    Were certain types of scene written particularly well? - For Example: sad scenes, tenser scenes, action scenes, mysterious ones...
    Did the story grip you and keep you turning the pages?

Writing-tips #3

Mention any dislikes of the book

Talk about why you think it didn't work for you. For example:

    If the ending was a cliffhanger, did you wish for a different ending because you found it frustrating?
    Did you find it hard to care for the main or any other characters? Could you work out why?
    Did the book focus on a theme you didn't found interesting or well written out?

Rounding up the review

Summarise your thoughts on the book by suggesting the type of reader you'd recommend the book to. For example: younger readers, older readers, fans of mystery/comedy/drama/etc. Are there any books or series you would compare it to?
